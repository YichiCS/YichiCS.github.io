Research Interests

I am committed to building trustworthy AI systems capable of handling complex tasks in critical scenarios. I believe it is essential to address three key factors: robustness, safety, and interpretability. Driven by these considerations, I aim to explore the following research questions:
• How can we ensure that models maintain sufficient robustness during interactions with external environments?
• How can we minimize bias and harmful content in model outputs to promote fairness and reliability?
• How can we establish comprehensive benchmarks that measures the trustworthiness LLMs from multiple dimensions?

In my past years of research experience, I have also explored these research directions:
AIGC Detection: How to quickly and accurately distinguish between real content and AI-generated content to protect property and personal safety from the harm of false information.
Backdoor Attack: How to design new backdoor attack paradigms to expose security vulnerabilities in machine learning models, providing new perspectives for secure and trustworthy defense mechanisms.
LLM Security: How to enhance the robustness of large language models to ensure their outputs are free from bias and harmful content, enabling their application in safety-critical complex tasks.
